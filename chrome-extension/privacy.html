🔒 Privacy Policy

Your Privacy, Our Core Principle

This document applies specifically to the Trust Less Sign Chrome Extension and explains how your data is collected, processed, and protected.

Last updated: June 20, 2026

🛡️
Zero-Trust Architecture

This extension operates on a zero-trust model. Your private keys and the contents of your PDF documents never leave your browser.

📡 1. Requested Permissions

The extension requests the following permissions with transparent and limited purposes:

storage Save your encrypted cryptographic keys locally.
identity Optional Google Drive integration.
activeTab Interact with PDFs when you initiate signing.

📋 2. Data Collection

  • Zero Data Collection — We do not collect or store any of your cryptographic material, passwords, or the contents of the documents you sign.
  • Local Storage — Your private key and certificate are stored exclusively in your browser's local storage and are encrypted at rest with a Master Password you define.

🚫 3. What the Extension Does NOT Do

  • Does not transmit your private keys or document contents to our servers or any third party.
  • Does not track your browsing history or activity.
  • Does not inject ads or track you across the web.
  • Does not read files in your Google Drive other than the documents you explicitly choose to sign.

🔐 4. Security & Cryptography

All document signing operations (hashing, signing, embedding QR codes) are performed locally inside your browser using the forge cryptography library and pdf-lib. Only the cryptographic public hashes are used for verification.
